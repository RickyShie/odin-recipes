Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is enjoyed by people all around the world. It is a delicious and hearty meal made up of layers of pasta, tomato sauce, meat, and cheese. The recipe typically involves boiling the lasagna noodles until they are al dente, browning meat with garlic and onions, and layering the noodles, meat sauce, and cheese into a baking dish. The dish is then baked until it is hot and bubbly, with a golden-brown crust on top. Lasagna is a great meal to serve for dinner parties, family gatherings, or just as a comforting weeknight meal.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ground meat
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese

Steps to make mouth-watering lasagna.

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until they are al dente, and then drain them and rinse with cold water.
  3. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground meat (usually beef or Italian sausage) with garlic and onions until browned. Add tomato sauce, salt, and pepper to taste, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, eggs, salt, pepper, and chopped herbs (usually parsley or basil).
  5. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Add a layer of lasagna noodles on top, then spread a layer of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles. Sprinkle grated mozzarella cheese on top of the ricotta layer. Repeat this layering process until all the ingredients are used up, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and mozzarella cheese on top.
  6. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the edges are golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
